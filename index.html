Are Horror Movies Getting Better?

BY Zach

Published July, 2018

We are amid what some are calling a Horror-Renaissance. In 2017, Get Out received a 99% rating on Rotten Tomatoes and received four Academy Awards nominations. After winning the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay, Get Out seemed to signal that horror movies will now be considered legitimate by mainstream critics. Again, in 2018, A Quiet Place received a 95% on Rotten Tomatoes and grossed $332.3 million on an estimated budget of $17-21 million. Is this influx of horror hits a recent surge, an anomaly, or a wave that has been building since the early 2000s? (probably, the last one)

Are horror movies getting better? By looking at Rotten Tomatoes Top 100 Horror Movies, we can try to determine if there is a trend in horror movie quality. The Rotten Tomatoes Top 100 Horror List features movies with 40 or more critics reviews and is ranked based on Adjusted Score based on how many ratings about the 40 minimum each movie has.

How Do I Get a Horror Movie in the Top 100?

If you want your horror movie to crack the list, you better include some Demons and a couple of monsters. Critics do not seem to be huge fans of aliens and zombies.

“None” generally included films that critics could categorize as psychological horror. For some reason, a large group of people seems to think that a sudden descent into madness is spookier than a ghost or chainsaw-wielding maniac.
